Investing 101

NJIT´s Training Program

Investing 101 is a 10-week training program created by the Investment Club with the collaboration of the School of Management where participants are introduced to the basics of investing. At the end of the program, the students who wish to become analysts of the club will be able to take a final exam where their abilities and skills will be measured. Any current student of the school is welcome to join us and learn the club´s investment philosophy. Some of the material given in this class will be based on The Five Rules for Succesful Stock Investing by Pat Dorsey.

    • 1. Introduction to Markets

    In this class the students will learn the principals of markets and economics. This class will include an overview of stocks, bonds, and other financial tools. In addition, the students will learn about inflation and interest rates.

    2. Financial Statements: Balance Sheet

    This class will cover an introduction of the Balance Sheet and its different accounts.

    3. Financial Statements: Income Statement

    This class will cover an introduction of the Income Statement and its different accounts.

    4. Financial Statements: Statement of Cash Flows

    This class will cover an introduction of the Statement of Cash Flows and its different accounts.

    5. Ratios

    In this class the students will become familiar with the different ratios that are used to measure the company´s financial statements.

    6. Discounted Cash Flow

    In this class the students will learn how to calculate the intrinsic value of a stock by using the DCF. The participants will also learn the advantages and disadvantages of using this method.

    7. Stock Sector

    In this class the participants will learn specific characteristics of each sector that NJIT is investing in.

    8. Portfolio

    In this class the students will learn how to calculate a portfolio return and risks and the importance of diversification

    9. Stock Picking Process: Developing your 10-minute Test

    In this class the students will learn to develop a ten minute test that will allow them to separate attractive companies that deserve a deeper analysis from non-attractive companies.

    10. Exam

    It is a written exam where the students will have to analyze a company´s financial statements and determine whether they would invest in the company or not.
